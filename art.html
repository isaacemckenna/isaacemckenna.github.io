"Communication Disco" (2022)

An installation at Brown University, December 2022. Consists of an audio-responsive projection shown through a mirror-shard chandelier, and includes an ambient soundtrack. The piece was meant to encourage audience participation.

I constructed the chandelier using fishing line, sanded-down mirror shards, wood, and a section of dragger net. These nets are often used on fishing boats, and are one of the most common ways that undersea fiber optic cables are damaged.

This piece was inspired by network disruption, with each material representing the infrastructure of the internet, including our exploitation of natural resources. The chandelier literally disrupts communication between users and the piece, and mistranscriptions from the responsive script enhance this effect.

"Cash back" (2022)

Set of two drawings done on thermal receipt paper using a hair dryer and a lighter, as well as water to prevent heat transfer. Process-driven piece, as well as an exploration into topics of economic exploitation and inequality.

"Clouds: Before/After" (2021)

This project is a set of 4 digital drawings created using a randomized script for Photoshop. The code chooses from a batch of hand-drawn circles and creates clumped patterns meant to resemble the negative space between clouds.

Each drawing has a corresponding poem, which were created with assistance from an AI text generator. The project addresses reduced cloud cover due to increased carbon dioxide in the atmosphere, and is meant to consider climate change from the perspective of a computer. It also brings up notions of authorship in an increasingly digital art space, blurring the line between my hand and my computer's.
